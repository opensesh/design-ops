# Proposal Writer

Create compelling project proposals for client work.

## Purpose

This skill produces professional, persuasive proposals that clearly communicate scope, value, and investment for design and development projects.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- Responding to RFPs or project inquiries
- Scoping new client engagements
- Creating SOWs (Statements of Work)
- Drafting project estimates with context

## Instructions

### Core Behavior

Effective proposals:
1. Lead with client goals, not your capabilities
2. Demonstrate understanding of their specific situation
3. Make scope and deliverables unambiguous
4. Present investment in context of value delivered

### Proposal Structure

```
1. Executive Summary (1 paragraph)
2. Understanding (their situation/goals)
3. Approach (how you'll solve it)
4. Scope & Deliverables (what they get)
5. Timeline (when they get it)
6. Investment (what it costs)
7. About Us (why you're qualified)
8. Next Steps (clear CTA)
```

### Process

1. **Discovery** - Gather project requirements and context
2. **Structure** - Outline using the proposal framework
3. **Draft** - Write each section with client focus
4. **Review** - Check for clarity and completeness
5. **Polish** - Ensure professional tone and formatting

### Writing Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|-----|-------|
| Use "you" and "your" liberally | Lead with "we" and "our" |
| Quantify outcomes when possible | Make vague value claims |
| Break down complex scopes | Use jargon without explanation |
| Include assumptions explicitly | Leave scope ambiguous |
| Provide options when appropriate | Present take-it-or-leave-it terms |

## Constraints

- Never include pricing without understanding scope requirements
- Avoid promising specific outcomes without caveats
- Don't make capability claims without supporting evidence
- Keep proposals under 10 pages unless complexity requires more

## Examples

### Example 1: Website Redesign Proposal

**Input:**
```
Create a proposal for a website redesign.
Client: Regional law firm, 15 attorneys
Goal: Modernize online presence, improve lead generation
Budget indication: $30-50K
Timeline: 3 months
```

**Output:**
```
# Website Redesign Proposal
## Anderson & Partners Legal

### Executive Summary
We propose a complete redesign of your firm's digital presence,
focused on establishing credibility with prospective clients and
converting visitors into consultations. The project spans 12 weeks
with an investment of $42,000.

### Understanding Your Situation
Your current website served the firm well when launched in 2018,
but the legal services landscape has shifted significantly:
- 76% of people seeking legal help start with online research
- Mobile traffic now exceeds desktop for legal services searches
- Prospective clients expect modern, professional digital experiences

You need a website that reflects your firm's expertise and makes
it easy for the right clients to reach you.

### Our Approach
We'll redesign your site with three priorities:

**1. Credibility First**
Attorney profiles, case results, and practice area depth that
demonstrate your expertise before a prospect ever calls.

**2. Clear Pathways**
Intuitive navigation that helps visitors find relevant information
and a frictionless path to scheduling consultations.

**3. Search Visibility**
Technical foundation and content structure optimized for local
search, ensuring you're found when it matters.

### Scope & Deliverables

**Discovery & Strategy (Weeks 1-2)**
- Stakeholder interviews
- Competitor analysis
- Content audit
- Information architecture

**Design (Weeks 3-5)**
- Visual design system
- Homepage and 3 template designs
- Mobile-responsive layouts
- 2 rounds of revisions

**Development (Weeks 6-10)**
- WordPress implementation
- Content migration
- Contact forms and intake integration
- Analytics setup

**Launch & Handoff (Weeks 11-12)**
- QA testing
- SEO verification
- Team training
- 30-day support period

### Investment
**Total: $42,000**

- Strategy & Discovery: $6,000
- Design: $14,000
- Development: $18,000
- Launch & Training: $4,000

Payment: 40% at signing, 30% at design approval, 30% at launch.

### Next Steps
1. Review this proposal with your partners
2. Schedule a call to discuss questions
3. Upon approval, we'll send a formal agreement
4. Kick off within 2 weeks of signed contract

We're excited about the opportunity to elevate Anderson & Partners'
digital presence.
```
