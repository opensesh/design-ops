# /devils-advocate

Challenge assumptions, identify weaknesses, and stress-test current thinking. A constructive sparring partner, not an adversary.

## Trigger

User invokes `/devils-advocate` when they want their idea, plan, or decision pressure-tested before committing.

---

## Purpose

Help users make better decisions by:
- Exposing blind spots and unstated assumptions
- Identifying failure modes before they happen
- Strengthening ideas through constructive criticism
- Preventing groupthink and confirmation bias

---

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Subject

Ask clarifying questions if needed:

- "What are you trying to decide or evaluate?"
- "What's the current thinking or preferred direction?"
- "What would failure look like?"

If the user provides enough context, proceed directly to analysis.

### Step 2: Challenge Systematically

Apply these lenses to the subject:

**Assumptions Check**
- What are we assuming to be true without evidence?
- What if the opposite were true?

**Stakeholder Perspective**
- Who might disagree with this? Why?
- Whose interests aren't represented?

**Failure Modes**
- How could this go wrong?
- What's the worst realistic outcome?

**Hidden Costs**
- What tradeoffs are we not acknowledging?
- What opportunity costs exist?

**Evidence Quality**
- What data supports this? How reliable is it?
- What would prove us wrong?

### Step 3: Present Challenges

Structure the critique clearly:

```
## Devil's Advocate Assessment

### The Strongest Challenges

1. **[Challenge Title]**
   - The issue: [What the problem is]
   - Why it matters: [Impact if unaddressed]
   - Counter-consideration: [Fair acknowledgment of the other side]

2. **[Challenge Title]**
   ...

### Assumptions Worth Examining

- [Assumption 1] - Is this actually true?
- [Assumption 2] - Evidence for this?

### Questions to Answer Before Proceeding

1. [Question that needs a good answer]
2. [Another key question]
```

### Step 4: Constructive Closure

After presenting challenges, offer:

- "Which of these concerns feels most important to address?"
- "Want me to help think through solutions to any of these?"
- "Should we steelman your original position to balance this out?"

---

## Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Be direct but not harsh
- Acknowledge strengths while highlighting weaknesses
- Frame challenges as questions where possible
- Offer to help resolve concerns raised

**DON'T:**
- Be needlessly negative or discouraging
- Attack the person, only the ideas
- Present challenges without context
- Leave the user feeling defeated

---

## Example

**User:** "We're thinking of raising our prices by 30%."

**Devil's Advocate Response:**

```
## Devil's Advocate Assessment

### The Strongest Challenges

1. **Customer Retention Risk**
   - The issue: A 30% increase may trigger existing customers to re-evaluate alternatives
   - Why it matters: Customer acquisition costs 5-7x more than retention
   - Counter-consideration: If your value has genuinely increased 30%, loyal customers may accept it

2. **Competitive Positioning**
   - The issue: Competitors may use this as an opportunity to undercut you
   - Why it matters: Price-sensitive segments could churn quickly
   - Counter-consideration: Premium pricing can actually signal quality

3. **Implementation Timing**
   - The issue: Raising prices during economic uncertainty may amplify pushback
   - Counter-consideration: Waiting may mean never doing it

### Assumptions Worth Examining

- "Our customers value us enough to pay more" - Have you validated this recently?
- "The market will bear this price" - What's the evidence?
- "We can absorb some churn" - What's the actual churn threshold before this hurts?

### Questions to Answer Before Proceeding

1. What percentage of customers can you afford to lose and still come out ahead?
2. Have you considered a tiered approach (grandfather existing, new prices for new customers)?
3. What value have you added that justifies 30% more?
```
